# USCIS N-400 official requirements snapshot

Source basis:

- USCIS Form N-400 Instructions, edition `01/20/25`, OMB expiry `02/28/2027`
- USCIS Form N-400, edition `01/20/25`

Use this knowledge only for USCIS filing guidance that appears in the official materials. If a question requires legal advice, case strategy, criminal analysis, travel risk analysis, tax strategy, or waivers outside the supported scope, the agent must say that a qualified immigration attorney or accredited representative should review it.

## General eligibility requirements from the USCIS instructions

For the general five-year permanent resident path, the official instructions say the applicant generally must:

- be at least 18 when filing
- have been a lawful permanent resident for at least 5 years
- show continuous residence in the United States for at least 5 years
- have lived for at least 3 months in the USCIS district or state where filing
- show physical presence in the United States for at least 30 months, listed as 913 days
- demonstrate good moral character
- show attachment to the principles of the U.S. Constitution
- demonstrate basic English and civics knowledge unless an exception applies
- be willing to take the Oath of Allegiance

## Early filing

- A five-year lawful permanent resident applicant may generally file up to 90 days before completing the full continuous-residence period.
- A spouse-of-U.S.-citizen applicant may generally file up to 90 days before completing the required 3-year continuous-residence period.
- The applicant still must satisfy all other requirements at the time of filing.
- If the applicant files too early, USCIS may reject the application.

## Filing instructions and evidence

- The applicant must complete the form fully and accurately.
- If a question does not apply, USCIS instructions say to enter `N/A` unless directed otherwise.
- If a numeric answer is zero or none, the instructions say to enter `None` unless directed otherwise.
- The applicant should submit required supporting evidence with the filing, although USCIS may accept some evidence at interview.
- USCIS may require biometrics.
- If documents are in a foreign language, a full English translation and translator certification are required.

## Signature rules

- USCIS does not accept a stamped or typewritten name in place of a required signature.
- A legal guardian, surrogate, or designated representative may sign in limited disability-related circumstances described by USCIS.

## Agent behavior rules based on the official instructions

- Never tell users they are definitely eligible. Say the app can help collect information and that USCIS makes the final decision.
- Never guess filing dates, physical-presence totals, or legal conclusions if the user gives incomplete facts.
- If the user asks whether they should file under a special category not fully supported in the app, the agent should explain the limitation and recommend legal review.
- If the user mentions arrests, crimes, long trips, unpaid taxes, false claims to citizenship, prior removal, selective service issues, or anything that may affect eligibility, the agent should stay factual, collect what the form asks, and recommend attorney review for legal consequences.
